# ABLE Account Qualified Expense Categories

ABLE (Achieving a Better Life Experience) accounts allow eligible individuals with disabilities to save and pay for qualified disability expenses without affecting eligibility for means-tested benefits like SSI and Medicaid.

The following 11 categories of qualified disability expenses are defined by the IRS under **Internal Revenue Code ยง529A** and related Treasury regulations.

> **Note:** This document is for reference purposes within the ABLE Tracker application. It is not tax or legal advice. For authoritative guidance, see [IRS Publication on ABLE Accounts](https://www.irs.gov/government-entities/federal-state-local-governments/able-accounts-tax-benefit-for-people-with-disabilities) and [26 U.S.C. ยง529A](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/529A).

---

## 1. Education

Expenses related to education at any level, from preschool through post-secondary and beyond.

**Examples:**
- Tuition and enrollment fees
- Books, supplies, and educational materials
- Tutoring services
- Costs related to special education programs

## 2. Housing

Expenses for housing, including rent, mortgage, and related costs.

**Examples:**
- Rent or mortgage payments
- Utilities (electric, gas, water, internet)
- Property taxes and homeowner's insurance
- Home modifications for accessibility (ramps, grab bars, widened doorways)

## 3. Transportation

Expenses for getting from place to place.

**Examples:**
- Vehicle purchase, lease, or modification for accessibility
- Rideshare services (Uber, Lyft) and taxi fares
- Public transit passes and paratransit fees
- Moving expenses related to a change of residence

## 4. Employment Training & Support

Expenses related to obtaining and maintaining employment.

**Examples:**
- Job coaching and supported employment services
- Vocational rehabilitation and training programs
- Resume writing and career counseling services
- Workplace accommodations and tools

## 5. Assistive Technology & Personal Support

Technology and personal services that assist with daily living.

**Examples:**
- Adaptive equipment (communication devices, mobility aids, screen readers)
- Personal care attendant services
- Service animal acquisition, training, and veterinary care
- Home automation and smart-home assistive devices

## 6. Health, Prevention & Wellness

Expenses for maintaining health and well-being.

**Examples:**
- Medical, dental, and vision care (copays, deductibles, procedures)
- Mental health services (therapy, counseling, psychiatric care)
- Prescription medications and medical supplies
- Gym memberships and wellness programs

## 7. Financial Management & Administrative

Expenses for managing finances and ABLE account administration.

**Examples:**
- ABLE account fees and maintenance charges
- Tax preparation services
- Financial planning and advisory fees
- Accounting and bookkeeping services

## 8. Legal Fees

Legal expenses related to disability needs.

**Examples:**
- Guardianship or conservatorship proceedings
- Estate planning (wills, trusts, special needs trusts)
- Disability-related legal advocacy and representation
- Appeals for denied benefits or accommodations

## 9. Oversight & Monitoring

Expenses for oversight and monitoring of the ABLE account beneficiary.

**Examples:**
- Professional monitoring services
- Care coordination and case management
- Expenses related to guardian or representative oversight duties
- Reporting and compliance-related costs

## 10. Funeral & Burial

End-of-life expenses for the account beneficiary.

**Examples:**
- Pre-paid funeral arrangements and contracts
- Burial or cremation expenses
- Cemetery plot and headstone costs
- Related memorial service expenses

## 11. Basic Living Expenses

Day-to-day costs of living.

**Examples:**
- Food and groceries
- Clothing and personal hygiene products
- Household supplies
- Other routine personal expenses

---

## Important Notes

- Qualified disability expenses must relate to the beneficiary's disability and be for the benefit of the beneficiary.
- Distributions for non-qualified expenses may be subject to income tax and a 10% penalty on the earnings portion.
- ABLE accounts are governed by IRC ยง529A. Each state administers its own ABLE program with its own rules and fee structures.
- When in doubt about whether an expense qualifies, consult a tax professional or the ABLE program administrator.
